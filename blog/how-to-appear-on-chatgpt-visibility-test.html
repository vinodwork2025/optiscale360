The 5-Minute AI Visibility Test (Step by Step)

This test checks your business across the five biggest AI search platforms. Follow each step exactly as written.

Step 1: Test ChatGPT

Open chat.openai.com in your browser. You can use the free version. Type a question that a real customer would ask about your type of business in your city.

Example queries to try:
"Who is the best [your service] in [your city]?"
"Recommend a good [your service] near [your area]"
"Top 5 [your service] providers in [your city]"

Run at least 3 different queries. Write down whether your business name appears in any of the responses. Also note which competitors ChatGPT mentions instead of you.

According to a February 2025 report by Similarweb, ChatGPT receives over 3 billion visits per month globally. A growing share of those users ask it for business recommendations. If you do not appear, those customers go to the businesses that do.

Step 2: Test Perplexity

Open perplexity.ai. Type the same queries you used in ChatGPT. Perplexity works differently because it searches the live web and cites its sources. Check two things: does your business name appear in the answer, and does Perplexity link to your website?

Perplexity is growing fast in India. According to its own published data from late 2024, the platform serves over 100 million queries per month. Many of those are local business queries.

Step 3: Test Google AI Overviews

Open Google on your phone and search for your service. Look at the top of the results page. If you see a box with an AI-generated summary before the regular search results, that is the AI Overview. Check if your business gets mentioned in that summary.

Not every search triggers an AI Overview. Try queries that start with "best", "top", or "recommend". These are the ones most likely to show AI-generated answers. Google rolled out AI Overviews across India in late 2024, and they now appear on a growing number of search queries.

Step 4: Test Bing AI and Gemini

Open bing.com/chat and run the same queries. Then open gemini.google.com and repeat. These platforms have smaller user bases in India, but they are growing. Testing them gives you the complete picture.

Why Most Indian Businesses Score 0 Out of 5

Most small and medium businesses in India score zero on this test. That is not because they are bad businesses. It is because AI search works completely differently from Google search. Here are the most common reasons businesses do not appear.

No schema markup on the website. Schema markup is code that tells AI tools what your business does, where you are located, and what services you offer. Without it, AI platforms cannot understand your business. A 2024 study by Schema App found that only 33% of websites use any schema markup at all. For Indian small businesses, that number is much lower.

Inconsistent business information. Your business name says "Kumar Consulting Pvt Ltd" on your website, "Kumar Consulting" on Google Business Profile, and "Kumar & Associates" on Justdial. AI tools see these as three different businesses. They lose confidence and skip you entirely.

No content that answers questions. AI tools look for content written in a question-and-answer format. If your website only has a homepage and a contact page, there is nothing for AI to extract and recommend.

According to a 2025 Botify study, websites with structured data and FAQ content are 40% more likely to appear in AI-generated search results compared to websites without these elements.

What to Do If You Scored Zero

A zero score is not permanent. Most businesses can start appearing on at least one AI platform within 2 to 8 weeks by making specific changes. Here is what works.

Fix 1: Add Schema Markup to Your Website

Add LocalBusiness or ProfessionalService schema to your homepage. Include your business name, address, phone number, services, and operating hours. Use the exact same information that appears on your Google Business Profile.

You can test your schema markup using Google's Rich Results Test. If the test shows errors or warnings, fix them.

Fix 2: Make Your NAP Data Identical Everywhere

NAP stands for Name, Address, and Phone number. Pick one exact format and use it everywhere. Your website, Google Business Profile, Justdial, Sulekha, IndiaMART, LinkedIn, and every other listing must show the exact same information.

For example, if your address is "13, Rayakotta Rd, Old Rayakottah Hudco, Hosur, Tamil Nadu 635109", do not write "No. 13, Rayakotta Road, Hosur" on another platform. AI tools treat small differences as signals of unreliability.

Fix 3: Add FAQ Content to Your Website

Write a list of 10 to 15 questions your customers actually ask. Answer each one clearly in 2 to 4 sentences. Add these to your website with proper FAQ schema markup.

Think about what someone in Bengaluru or Hosur would type into ChatGPT when looking for your service. Those exact questions should be on your website with clear answers.

Fix 4: Complete Your Google Business Profile

Fill in every single field. Add your services, business description, photos, operating hours, and at least 5 customer reviews. Google AI Overviews pull heavily from Google Business Profile data. An incomplete profile means zero AI visibility on Google.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check if my business appears on ChatGPT?

Open ChatGPT and type a question a real customer would ask. For example, "Who is the best interior designer in Hosur?" or "Best CA firm near Bengaluru." If your business name does not appear in the response, you have an AI visibility problem. Test at least 5 different queries to get a clear picture.

Can I pay to appear on ChatGPT?

No. ChatGPT does not sell paid placements. It recommends businesses based on how well it understands them from publicly available information. You need structured data on your website, consistent business listings, and content that directly answers customer questions. There is no shortcut you can buy.

Why does my competitor appear on ChatGPT but I don't?

Your competitor likely has better structured data (schema markup) on their website, more consistent business listings across directories, and content that answers questions in a clear format. AI tools pick up on these signals. The good news is that most of these fixes take days, not months.

How long does it take to appear on ChatGPT after making changes?

Most businesses start seeing results within 2 to 8 weeks after making the right changes. ChatGPT and other AI tools update their knowledge regularly. Perplexity pulls live data, so changes there can show up within days. Google AI Overviews also update frequently as Google recrawls your site.

Does Google AI Overview show different results than ChatGPT?

Yes. Each AI platform uses different data sources and ranking signals. Your business might appear on Google AI Overviews but not on ChatGPT. Or it might show up on Perplexity but nowhere else. That is why you need to test across all five major platforms to understand your full AI visibility.

Is AI search visibility important for small businesses in India?

Very important. ChatGPT has millions of users in India. Perplexity is growing fast. Google AI Overviews now appear on many Indian search queries. When a customer in Bengaluru or Hosur asks an AI tool to recommend a service, only 3 to 5 businesses get named. If you are not one of them, that customer goes to your competitor.

What is the difference between SEO and AI search visibility?

SEO focuses on ranking in Google's list of ten blue links. AI search visibility focuses on getting your business recommended directly by AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity. SEO relies on backlinks and keywords. AI visibility relies on structured data, entity clarity, and content that answers questions directly. You need both.
