How AI search is different from Google search

Traditional Google search returns a list of links. The user clicks through to find an answer. AI search is different. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity a question, the AI generates a direct answer in plain language and may include a short list of businesses or sources it considers relevant.

This changes the dynamic for businesses significantly. With Google, you competed to appear in a list of ten results per page. With AI search, there may be two or three businesses mentioned in an entire response, or none at all. The AI either knows about your business or it does not. There is no page two.

Google AI Overviews, which appear at the top of Google search results in India, operate similarly. They generate a synthesised answer and occasionally cite specific businesses that meet their threshold for relevance and trustworthiness.

Which AI platforms matter in India right now

These are the platforms where Indian consumers are increasingly turning for business recommendations:

  • ChatGPT: The most used AI assistant globally, with significant adoption in Indian metros. Business-to-business queries are especially common here.
  • Perplexity: Preferred by users who want cited sources alongside AI-generated answers. Perplexity searches the web in real time and often cites specific websites.
  • Google AI Overviews: Appearing at the top of Google results in India. These reach the largest audience because they appear within Google's existing search interface.
  • Gemini: Google's AI assistant, increasingly integrated into Google Search and Google Workspace products used by Indian businesses.
  • Bing Copilot: Microsoft's AI search, relevant particularly for Windows users and enterprise environments.

You do not need to optimise separately for each platform. The underlying factors that improve visibility on one tend to improve visibility on all of them.

Why most Indian businesses are invisible on AI search

AI engines build their knowledge from publicly available web content. For a business to appear in AI-generated answers, three things generally need to be true:

  1. The business has enough content online for the AI to understand what it does and where it operates.
  2. That content is structured in a way AI can interpret accurately.
  3. The business is mentioned on sources other than its own website, giving the AI corroboration that it is legitimate.

Most Indian business websites fail on all three. A typical website has thin service descriptions, no structured data, and few external mentions. The AI literally has nothing to cite.

This is not unique to India, but the gap is larger here because many Indian businesses built their web presence for Google's 2015 era algorithm and have not updated it since. The internet moved on. The websites did not.

What AI visibility actually means

Being visible on AI search means one or more of the following:

  • Your business is named when someone asks an AI for recommendations in your category and location.
  • Your website is cited as a source when an AI explains a topic related to your services.
  • Your content appears in Google AI Overviews when users search for relevant queries.
  • Your Google Business Profile data is used by AI engines to answer local business queries.

It does not mean your business ranks first for every query. It means you are within the set of businesses the AI considers credible enough to mention. Once you are in that set, query-level placement varies based on factors like the user's specific location, the exact question asked, and how your business compares to others in the same category.

The five factors that determine AI search visibility

1. Content depth

AI engines cannot cite what they cannot understand. A service page with 80 words gives an AI almost no information. A service page that explains what you do, who you serve, your process, your pricing range, and your service area gives the AI everything it needs to recommend you accurately.

Target a minimum of 500 words for each core service page. This is not about hitting a word count. It is about covering the topic completely enough that a reader or an AI can understand exactly what you offer.

2. Factual specificity

Vague language is invisible to AI. "We have years of experience" means nothing. "We have been handling GST filing and income tax returns for small businesses in Hosur since 2018" is specific, verifiable, and citable.

Every claim on your website should be concrete. Instead of "affordable pricing," write "our packages start from Rs. 8,000 per month." Instead of "serving clients across South India," write "we work with clients in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh, primarily in the manufacturing and retail sectors."

3. Structured data

Schema markup is code added to your website that tells AI engines and search engines exactly what your business is. Without it, AI must infer your business type from your text alone, which is unreliable. With it, you are providing a structured, machine-readable description.

At minimum, Indian businesses should have LocalBusiness or ProfessionalService schema with their full address, phone number, opening hours, and service area. FAQPage schema on pages with FAQ sections is also important because it maps directly to the question-answering behaviour of AI engines.

4. NAP consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. AI engines cross-reference your business details across multiple sources: your website, your Google Business Profile, JustDial, Sulekha, and other directories. If these details are inconsistent, the AI's confidence in your business data drops.

Check that your business name, address, and phone number are identical everywhere they appear online. Even small variations such as "No. 13, Rayakotta Road" on one site and "13 Rayakotta Rd" on another can create confusion in AI data models.

5. Third-party mentions

AI engines trust businesses that are mentioned by other sources. This is similar to how a reference from a trusted person carries more weight than your own self-introduction. Directory listings, reviews, mentions in local news, and citations on industry websites all contribute to this.

For Indian businesses, the most accessible third-party mentions come from Google Business Profile reviews, JustDial listings, trade association directories, and local business news coverage.

GEO vs SEO: what is different and what overlaps

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation. It refers to the practice of making your content and online presence optimised for AI-generated answers, as opposed to traditional SEO which optimised for position in a list of search results.

There is significant overlap. Good content, accurate business information, and a trustworthy web presence help both SEO and GEO. The differences are:

  • Keywords vs. topics: SEO focuses on specific keywords. GEO focuses on complete topic coverage because AI answers questions holistically rather than matching exact keyword strings.
  • Backlinks vs. mentions: SEO treats backlinks as a primary ranking factor. GEO values any credible mention of your business, not just links.
  • Page ranking vs. citation: SEO gets you to page one of results. GEO gets you cited in a generated answer. These require different content structures.
  • Featured snippets matter more: Content formatted to answer specific questions directly (FAQ format, definition blocks, step-by-step processes) performs better for GEO.

If you are starting from scratch on AI visibility, the fastest path is to address content depth, structured data, and NAP consistency simultaneously. These have high impact and low cost.

How long it takes to see results

AI visibility does not update in real time. AI engines process web content on their own schedule, and changes you make to your website today will typically take 4 to 8 weeks to be reflected in AI-generated answers.

Google AI Overviews update more frequently because they tie into Google's regular crawl cycle. If you have a verified Google Business Profile and your website is indexed by Google, changes you make to structured data and content can start appearing in Google AI Overviews within 2 to 4 weeks.

Perplexity searches the web in real time for some queries, which means recently updated content can appear faster. However, your domain authority and the credibility of your website still affect whether Perplexity chooses to cite you.

Priority actions for Indian businesses

If you want to improve your AI search visibility, start with these in order:

  1. Verify and complete your Google Business Profile. This is the single highest-impact action. A complete, verified Google Business Profile feeds data directly into multiple AI platforms. Make sure your category, description, hours, photos, and service area are all accurate and current.
  2. Rewrite your main service pages. Each service page should be at least 500 words and should explain what you do, who you serve, your process, your pricing range, and your location.
  3. Add FAQ sections to your home page and service pages. Write the questions your clients actually ask and answer each one in a full paragraph.
  4. Add LocalBusiness schema markup to your website. Include your full address, phone, email, and service area.
  5. Check NAP consistency across JustDial, Sulekha, and your website. Fix any variations in your business name, address, or phone number.
  6. Run an AI visibility test. Search for your business category and city on ChatGPT and Perplexity. Note which competitors appear and which do not. This tells you the baseline you are working from.

These steps are not quick fixes. They require honest effort and time. But they are the foundation of AI visibility, and every subsequent improvement builds on them.

Frequently asked questions

Do I still need traditional SEO if I focus on AI search?

Yes. Traditional SEO and AI search visibility share most of the same foundations: quality content, accurate business information, and a technically sound website. Doing one well tends to support the other. The difference is in emphasis, not direction.

Does AI search matter for my industry or is it just for tech companies?

AI search is relevant for any business that receives queries through search. We have seen AI visibility matter for CA firms, interior designers, construction companies, manufacturers, logistics businesses, and healthcare providers in India. If your customers search for services like yours online, AI search matters for your business.

How much does it cost to improve AI visibility?

The content and structural changes described in this guide can be done without any tool subscriptions. The cost is primarily your time. A professional AI visibility audit from OptiScale Advisors starts at Rs. 15,000 and delivers a detailed report with specific fixes within 48 hours.

Will AI search replace Google?

Not in the immediate future. Google remains the dominant search platform in India by a wide margin. However, AI features are being integrated into Google itself through AI Overviews, and a growing segment of users, particularly younger professionals, are switching to AI-first search tools for research and recommendations. Building AI visibility now positions your business well regardless of how the search landscape shifts.
