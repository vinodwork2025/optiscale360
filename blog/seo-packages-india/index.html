SEO Packages in India: Where Your Money Actually Goes

By Vinod Kumar | OptiScale Advisors | June 2026 | 7 min read

Every SEO package in India hides the same secret. The price tells you almost nothing. What matters is the hours of real work behind it, and most agencies pray you never ask.

This post shows you the costing sheet. What a package actually buys at each price, hour by hour. Once you see it, you'll never get fooled by a cheap quote again.

How much do SEO packages cost in India?

Short answer. Genuine SEO packages in India run ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 a month for local and small businesses, ₹25,000 to ₹60,000 for competitive markets, and ₹50,000 to ₹2,00,000 or more for ecommerce and national brands. Anything under ₹8,000 a month is usually automated junk wearing a package label.

Now the part nobody shows you. Why.

SEO is hours, not magic

Strip away the jargon and an SEO package is just skilled hours. Someone researching what your buyers search. Someone writing pages worth reading. Someone fixing your site's speed and structure. Someone earning links from real websites. Someone checking what moved and why.

A competent SEO person in India costs an agency roughly ₹300 to ₹800 an hour once you count salary, tools, and overheads. Good writers and technical people cost more. Hold that number. It explains every quote you'll ever get.

The ₹5,000 package, opened up

Say you pay ₹5,000 a month. The agency keeps maybe half as profit and overhead. That leaves ₹2,500 of actual work. At real labour rates, that's three to five hours. A month.

What fits in five hours? Not research. Not writing. Not technical fixes. So they use shortcuts: software that blasts your link to spam directories, copied content with your city name swapped in, and a monthly "ranking report" for keywords nobody searches.

You're not buying SEO. You're buying a report that says SEO happened. And the spam links can earn you a Google penalty that costs more to undo than honest work would have cost from day one.

If ₹5,000 is truly your budget, don't buy a monthly package at all. Pay once for a proper audit and a few key pages done right. One month of real work beats a year of theatre.

The ₹20,000 package, opened up

Now the same exercise at ₹20,000. After agency overheads, you're getting roughly 20 to 30 real hours. Here's what an honest month looks like:

Four to six hours on your money pages. Titles, headings, internal links, and schema markup done properly.

Eight to twelve hours on content. One or two genuinely useful pages or posts written for your buyers. This is the engine of the whole thing, and it's where cheap packages quietly put nothing.

Four to six hours on technical health. Speed, mobile experience, indexing, broken links.

Three to five hours earning links. A few real ones from relevant sites. Slow, manual, and worth ten times a bag of spam links.

Two hours on tracking and a report you can actually read.

That's a real package. Notice there's no slack in it. Which is exactly why a ₹5,000 quote can't be doing these things. The hours don't exist.

The ₹50,000-plus tier

Above ₹50,000 you're buying scale, not different work. More content every month, digital PR for stronger links, deeper technical management across hundreds of pages. Ecommerce sites and national brands need this. A local business almost never does. If you run a clinic or a workshop and someone quotes you ₹60,000, they're selling you a truck to carry a lunchbox.

The four questions that expose any package

Forget comparing prices. Compare hours and proof.

How many hours of work does this package include each month? Watch them squirm. Honest agencies can answer.

What content will you produce, and who writes it? Ask for a sample. Content is most of the value.

Show me last month's work for any client, names hidden. Real work leaves a trail. Theatre doesn't.

What happens if I stop paying? Lock-in contracts protect lazy agencies. Month-to-month keeps everyone honest.

The blind spot in almost every package: AI search

Here's what even many expensive packages still ignore. Close to 60% of Google searches now end without a click because Google's AI answers the question on the spot. And your buyers have started asking ChatGPT and Perplexity directly: "best packaging supplier near Bangalore," "reliable web design company in Tamil Nadu."

If AI engines can't see your business, you're missing the fastest-growing slice of search. Making you visible there is specific work: clean schema, clear signals about who you are and where you operate, and pages structured so AI can quote them.

Simple test. Ask any agency, "What exactly will you do to get me visible in AI search?" Silence tells you their package was designed five years ago.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does SEO cost per month in India?

Most genuine SEO work in India costs ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 a month for small and local businesses. Competitive markets run ₹25,000 to ₹60,000. Ecommerce and national campaigns go from ₹50,000 up past ₹2,00,000. The price reflects hours of skilled work, so quotes far below these ranges usually mean the hours don't exist.

Why is some SEO so cheap at ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 a month?

Because almost no work happens. After agency profit and overheads, ₹5,000 leaves about three to five hours of labour a month. That only covers automated link spam, copied content, and a ranking report for keywords nobody searches. It can also earn your site a Google penalty.

How many hours of work should an SEO package include?

A real package for a small business should include 20 to 30 hours of work a month. That covers content writing, on-page fixes, technical health checks, link building, and reporting. Ask any agency this question directly. Honest ones can answer in seconds.

How long does SEO take to show results in India?

Expect early movement in 2 to 4 months and meaningful enquiries in 4 to 8 months, depending on competition and your site's condition. Anyone promising page one in 30 days is lying. But "SEO takes time" is not an excuse for zero progress. You should see work delivered and small wins every single month.

Is a 12-month SEO contract normal?

It's common, but you should refuse it. Long lock-ins remove the agency's reason to perform. Month-to-month agreements keep the work honest because the agency has to earn the renewal. Any agency confident in its work doesn't need to trap you.

What should an SEO package include?

Six things at minimum. A technical audit with fixes, keyword research tied to your buyers, on-page optimization with schema markup, fresh content every month, links earned manually from real sites, and a plain-language monthly report. If content is missing, the package is mostly empty.

What is AI search optimization and do I need it?

AI search optimization makes your business visible when people ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI for recommendations. It involves schema markup, clear business signals, and content structured so AI can quote it. You need it because close to 60% of Google searches now end without a click, and buyers increasingly ask AI tools directly who to hire.

Can I do SEO myself instead of paying an agency?

Yes, for the basics. You can claim your Google Business Profile, collect reviews, write honest pages about your services, and keep your site fast. What's hard to do yourself is technical SEO, schema, and link building. Many small businesses do the basics themselves and hire help only for the technical layer.

Should I hire a freelancer or an agency for SEO?

Freelancers in India typically charge ₹10,000 to ₹40,000 a month and suit small, focused projects. Agencies charge more but bring a team across content, technical, and links. Judge either one the same way: hours delivered, work shown, no lock-in. A good freelancer beats a lazy agency every time.

How do I check if my SEO agency is actually working?

Ask for the list of work done each month in plain words: pages written, fixes made, links earned. Check your Google Search Console yourself, it's free and shows real clicks and impressions. And test your business in ChatGPT or Perplexity. If months pass with no list, no movement, and no visibility, you're paying for theatre.
