What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit them. They help websites remember information about your visit, like your preferences and actions. Cookies make your browsing experience smoother and more personalized.

Think of cookies like a library card—they help the website recognize you when you return and remember where you left off.

Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Remember your preferences – So you don't have to re-enter information on every visit
  • Understand how you use our site – To improve your experience and make our content more useful
  • Analyze website performance – To see what's working well and what needs improvement
  • Provide better service – To personalize content and functionality for you
  • Track conversions – To measure the effectiveness of our marketing efforts

Types of Cookies We Use

1. Essential Cookies (Required)

These cookies are necessary for our website to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation, form submissions, and security. Without these cookies, our website can't work correctly.

You cannot disable essential cookies because they're required for the website to function. However, they don't collect personal information for marketing purposes.

2. Analytics Cookies (Optional)

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use Google Analytics to track:

  • Number of visitors to our site
  • Which pages are most popular
  • How long people stay on each page
  • Where visitors come from (search engines, social media, etc.)
  • What devices and browsers people use

3. Functionality Cookies (Optional)

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (like your language preference or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features. They may also remember changes you've made to text size, fonts, and other customizable parts of our website.

4. Marketing Cookies (Optional)

These cookies track your online activity to help us deliver more relevant advertising. They remember websites you've visited and share this information with other parties like advertisers. We use these to:

  • Show you relevant ads on other websites
  • Measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns
  • Limit the number of times you see the same ad

How to Control Cookies

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Clear all cookies when you close your browser
  • Set exceptions for specific websites

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies

Warning: Blocking all cookies may prevent some features of our website from working properly. Some services, like our booking system and contact forms, require cookies to function.

Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific tracking services:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on
  • Advertising: Visit Digital Advertising Alliance or Your Online Choices

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that lets you tell websites you don't want to be tracked. However, there's no industry standard for how websites should respond to these signals. Currently, our website doesn't respond to Do Not Track signals, but we respect your right to control cookies through other means described above.

Mobile Devices

Mobile devices use identifiers rather than cookies. You can control these through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Advertising → Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization
