How Much Do SEO Packages Cost in India?

Most genuine SEO packages in India cost between ₹10,000 and ₹50,000 per month. Small and local businesses usually pay ₹10,000 to ₹25,000. Businesses in competitive markets pay ₹25,000 to ₹60,000. Ecommerce and large national campaigns run ₹50,000 to ₹2,00,000 or more.

Anything under ₹8,000 a month is almost always junk work dressed up as a package. More on that below.

What you are Realistic monthly range What it should cover
Local shop, clinic, small service business ₹10,000 – ₹25,000 Google Business Profile, local pages, citations, on-page fixes
Growing SME in a competitive market ₹25,000 – ₹60,000 Content every month, link building, technical SEO, reporting
Ecommerce or national brand ₹50,000 – ₹2,00,000+ Large-scale content, digital PR, full technical management

These are market ranges, not our price list. Quotes vary by city, competition, and how much work your site actually needs.

Why the Prices Vary So Much

SEO cost is mostly labour. A proper package needs hours of real work every month: research, writing, fixing your site, earning links, checking results. Good people cost money.

So when two quotes are far apart, they're not selling the same thing. A ₹5,000 package and a ₹30,000 package are as different as a ₹2,000 phone and a ₹50,000 phone. Both are technically phones.

Competition. Ranking a bakery in Hosur is cheap. Ranking a loan website nationally is war.

Your site's condition. A slow site with no structure needs repair work before SEO can even start.

Content needs. If your package includes real articles and pages every month, that's most of the cost. Packages with no content are mostly empty.

The Cheap SEO Trap

Here's the math nobody shows you. Say an agency charges ₹5,000 a month. After their profit and overheads, maybe ₹2,500 of actual work happens on your site. That buys an hour or two of a junior's time, or worse, automated software blasting spam links.

What you actually get at that price: copied content, directory spam, fake "ranking reports" for keywords nobody searches, and links from junk sites. Best case, nothing happens. Worst case, Google penalises your site, and recovering from that costs far more than honest SEO would have.

If ₹5,000 a month is genuinely your full budget, don't buy a cheap package. Spend it once on a proper audit and a few fixed pages done well. One month of real work beats a year of fake work.

What a Real Package Must Include

Whatever you pay, these six things should be in it. If any are missing, ask why.

  • A technical audit and fixes. Speed, mobile, indexing, broken pages. This is the foundation.
  • Keyword research tied to your buyers. Not a list of 50 random words. The actual phrases your customers type.
  • On-page work. Titles, headings, internal links, and schema markup done properly on your money pages.
  • Content. Real pages and posts written for your buyers, not filler. This is where most of the budget should go.
  • Links earned the honest way. A few good links beat a hundred spammy ones.
  • A monthly report you can understand. Enquiries and rankings that matter, not a 30-page PDF of jargon.

Red Flags Before You Pay Anyone

Walk away if you see these.

  • "Guaranteed #1 on Google." Nobody controls Google. Anyone promising a rank is lying or planning to rank you for your own company name, which you'd rank for anyway.
  • Pricing per keyword. "10 keywords for ₹6,000." Real SEO doesn't work per keyword. It's a sign of template junk work.
  • 12-month lock-in contracts. SEO takes months to show results, true. But a lock-in removes their reason to perform. Month-to-month keeps them honest.
  • They won't show you the work. If they can't list what they did this month in plain words, they didn't do much.
  • Their own website is weak. An SEO company that doesn't rank, has no schema on its own site, and loads slowly is telling you exactly what you'll get.
  • Secret methods. "We can't reveal our techniques." Real SEO has no secrets, only effort. Secrecy usually hides spam.

Questions to Ask Any SEO Company

Five questions, two minutes, and you'll know who you're dealing with.

  1. What exactly will you do in month one? Vague answers mean vague work.
  2. Can I see a report you sent another client? Names hidden is fine. You want to see how they communicate.
  3. Who writes the content, and can I see a sample? Content quality decides whether SEO works.
  4. What happens if I want to stop? Listen for lock-ins and exit penalties.
  5. How will you measure success? If the answer is "rankings" and not "enquiries," they're optimizing for the wrong thing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good budget for SEO in India?

For most small businesses, ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 a month is a realistic working budget. Local businesses with light competition sit at the lower end. If your market is crowded, plan for ₹25,000 to ₹60,000. Below ₹8,000 a month, genuine work is rarely possible.

Are cheap SEO packages worth it?

No. Packages under ₹8,000 a month usually rely on automated links and copied content. Best case, nothing improves. Worst case, Google penalises your site and recovery costs more than honest SEO would have. If your budget is small, spend it once on an audit and a few well-built pages instead.

What is included in a monthly SEO package?

A genuine package covers a technical audit and fixes, keyword research, on-page optimization with schema markup, fresh content each month, manually earned links, and a clear monthly report. Content should take the largest share of the budget because it does most of the ranking work.

How is SEO priced in India: monthly, hourly, or per project?

Three models exist. Monthly retainers suit ongoing growth and are the most common. Project pricing suits one-time jobs like an audit or site migration. Hourly consulting suits specific questions. Per-keyword pricing is the fourth model you'll see offered, and it's the one to refuse. Real SEO doesn't work per keyword.

Do SEO companies guarantee first page rankings?

Honest ones don't, because nobody controls Google. A company guaranteeing #1 is either lying or planning to rank you for your own brand name, which you'd rank for anyway. The only guarantees worth anything are about work delivered and defined, measurable outcomes agreed in writing.

Is local SEO cheaper than national SEO?

Yes, usually by a lot. Local SEO for one city runs ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 a month because you're competing with a handful of nearby businesses. National campaigns cost ₹50,000 and up because you're fighting every competitor in the country plus large brands with big budgets.

How do I compare two SEO quotes?

Ignore the price difference and compare deliverables. Ask both for the exact monthly work list: how many pages of content, what technical fixes, how many links and from where, what reporting. Then ask each what they'll do for AI search visibility. The quote that answers in specifics wins, almost regardless of price.

If your business isn't showing up in AI search for those queries within 90 days, we keep working free for up to 90 more days until it does.

Does GST apply to SEO services in India?

Yes. SEO services attract 18% GST. When comparing quotes, check whether the price is inclusive or exclusive of GST. A registered business can claim the input credit, so always insist on a proper GST invoice. An agency that can't give one is a red flag in itself.

When should I stop paying for SEO?

Stop when months pass with no work shown, no movement in Google Search Console, and only excuses in the reports. Don't stop just because results are gradual. Real SEO compounds, and quitting at month three often wastes the foundation you paid for. Judge the work first, the results curve second.
